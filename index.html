1850s


European immigrants came to settle on Barren Island, Brooklyn, near what is now known The Dead Horse Bay. All of them worked as laborers at the horse & animal rendering plants where dead animals are processed into soap, oil, candles, etc.

1890s


The New York Sanitary Utilization Company opened on Barren Island, turning it into NYC's garbage dump. From then, the residents indured horrifying strenches everyday.

1920s


Robert Morses' city project took place, residents were forced to move out to make room for highways and the Floyd Bennett Field airport.

Today


As a result of poverty, many residents had to dump all of their belongs onto the beach, resulting in today's Dead Horse Bay full of objects from the 1920s.
